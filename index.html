About Me

I’m Christopher, a retired Army aviation mechanic with over two decades of experience maintaining critical systems in high-stakes environments. My career has been defined by precision, teamwork, and a sense of purpose, qualities I now bring to the field of software engineering.

Programming has been a passion of mine even before I started my aviation career. After retiring, I decided to pursue it professionally. I am drawn to coding because of the creativity and problem-solving it demands. There is nothing quite like seeing your ideas come to life or tackling a challenging problem and finding the solution.

Currently, I’m earning my degree in Software Engineering at WGU while refining my skills in C#, modern UI frameworks, and cross-platform development. My goal is to build a career that offers flexibility and balance, allowing me to create impactful solutions while maintaining quality of life.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing guitar and spending time with my family. Both keep me grounded and inspired as I navigate this exciting new chapter.
